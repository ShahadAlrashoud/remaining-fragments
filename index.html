About This Research

Your past doesn't
disappear. It persists.

This tool is a research prototype built alongside the paper "Remaining Fragments: Reconstructing Digital Identities Through Passive Browsing."

The study found that comprehensive digital profiles can be reconstructed through passive, non-technical browsing alone — no hacking, no specialized tools. A creator with a small local following yielded nearly the same depth of recoverable data as an internationally recognized figure.

Digital exposure is a function of participation, not prominence.

Key Findings
  • // Finding 01
    Profiles spanning 5+ years of activity were fully reconstructable through publicly accessible platforms with no specialized tools.
  • // Finding 02
    Abandoned handles from years ago still resolve to active indexed profiles — including full rebranding timelines and old usernames.
  • // Finding 03
    Cross-platform username consistency makes aggregation trivial — a single search reconstructs posting frequency and behavioral patterns.
  • // Finding 04
    Saudi Arabia's PDPL marks regulatory progress, but digital footprint management remains significantly under-addressed in digital citizenship education.
